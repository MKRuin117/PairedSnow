Employment

Looking to join us? We'd love to have you! Here's a guide on how to apply:

  1. Contact us at snowresortohiobusiness@gmail.com
  2. Attatch a digital copy of your resume and any other relevant information as well as a brief summary of your abilities
  3. We'll contact you back in 2-5 business days depending on how busy we are
  4. Once we contact you, we'll schedule an in-person interview at our Recon Center at the base of the mountain
  5. Bring a printed copy of your resume and any other relevant information with you to the interview
  6. Expect to recieve news about your application up to two weeks after the interview

Currently Available Positions

  1. Skiing Coach
  2. Trail Ranger
  3. Trail Maintainance Worker
  • Skiing Coach
  • Experience

    You must have consderable experience in skiing and must be a certified coach. Bring this certification during your interview.


  • Positivity

    We take pride in having interactive and positive coaches. To be a coach you need to have patience and provide a healthy learning environment while keeping it engaging and fun for the people you're teaching.


  • Care

    To be a coach is to also take on other jobs if you are needed. You may, on rare occasions, be asked to tend to the different courses or scope out potential hazards alongside our maintainance team.


  • Trail Ranger
  • Ranger's Oath

    "We promise to uphold the integrity of our company, the trails and the people therein. We swear to treat trails, hikers, staff and wildlife with respect and dignity." This is our Ranger's Oath, and every Ranger must take this oath upon being hired, and will be held to its standards accordingly.


  • Emergency Training

    Unfortunately, accidents and emergencies happen on the trails. You must be trained to handle them. You will be provided with an emergency medical kit, a non-lethal rifle with rubber bullets in case of hostile wildlife, and an emergency survival kit consisting of a survival multitool, disposable firestarter, tinderbox and signal flare. You must be trained in the use of all of the items listed above. In an emergency, you must remain calm and console the hikers if needed. You will provide guidance and do your utmost best to lead them back to the nearest Ranger station, information kiosk or other safe area where help can be recieved.


  • Wilderness Experience

    It goes without saying that you need to be a good at hiking, but you need more than just that. You need to have knowledge of botany, differnet types of local wildlife and survival training. Aforementioned experience will serve you well, and you'll no doubt need it on the trails.



  • Trail & Course Maintainance Worker
  • Geographical Knowledge

    To maintain the courses and trails, you must know them. You must get a lay of the land: which rocks are safe to climb, what places are safe to go, and much more. Bring evidence of this training to your interview (i.e: a certificate or degree). Your job is to keep the trails and courses as safe as possible.


  • Humility

    On the maintainance team, you'll be required to do the dirty work; to maintain the place and keep it up to scratch. Cleaning, low-risk maintainance and other mundane tasks are all part of the job. Don't worry, though, you'll be accompanied by at least one other person and will never be sent out on your own due to company policy.


  • Regulations

    As a maintainance worker, you have various items that can be used to temporarily seal off unsafe areas. If you deem an area unsafe, you can use light posts, caution tape and signs to warn hikers and skiiers not to go there. If you think a course needs to be closed for maintainance, please contact a superior.
